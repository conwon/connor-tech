Astronauts And Space Exploration

by Connor Wabst

Astronauts

An Astronaut is a person who goes to explore the great vacuum of space. Some other names for the word Astronaut are "cosmonaut" in Russia or in China they say "Taikonaut".

The first astronaut to enter space was a cosmonaut named Yuri Gagarin on April 12, 1961.

The first woman in space was also a cosmonaut named Valentina Tereshkova on June 16, 1963.

Astronauts (USA!)

The second astronaut and first American in space was Alan Shephard. He orbited around the planet just like Yuri Gagarin did a couple months ago and entered space in May 5, 1961.

The first American woman in space was Sally Ride in 1983 aboard the famous Space Shuttle Challenger which later blew up in a disaster in 1986.

Astronauts!

Astronauts come from all over from many backgrounds. Some have been pilots, scientists, and even teachers.

Astronaut Country Born Notes

Spacecraft

The first manned spacecraft was Vostok 1, which carried Soviet cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin

The first American spacecraft was Mercury-Redstone which was a Mercury capsule for the astronaut(s) on top of the Redstone rocket launch vehicle.
